# Cold Email Teardown Call Script

**Duration:** 15 minutes  
**Goal:** Deliver immediate value + pitch paid 90-min strategy session ($200)  
**Format:** Zoom or phone call  

---

## Pre-Call Setup (3 minutes before)

1. **Review their profile:**
   - Check LinkedIn for recent posts/company updates
   - Look at their company website
   - Find any cold emails they've sent (search LinkedIn messages, their website contact form emails, or ask them to forward one)

2. **Have ready:**
   - Notepad for live notes
   - Screen share ready (if Zoom)
   - Calendar link for paid session: [Insert Calendly/booking link]

---

## Call Structure

### Opening (2 minutes)

**You say:**
> "Hey [FirstName], thanks for jumping on! So this is a quick 15-minute teardown — I'm going to review one of your cold emails, point out 3 things you could fix immediately, and leave you with something actionable. Sound good?"

**Wait for confirmation.**

> "Perfect. Do you have a recent cold email you've sent that I can look at? If you can drop it in the Zoom chat or forward it to me now, that's ideal. Or you can just read me the subject line and first 3 sentences."

**Get the email.** If they don't have one handy:
> "No problem — tell me: who are you selling to, and what's the outcome they care about?"

---

### Teardown (8–10 minutes)

**Give them 3 fixes on the spot.** Use the framework below. Be specific, direct, and actionable.

#### Fix 1: Subject Line
**Most common issues:**
- Too generic ("Improve your [X]")
- Too long (>7 words)
- Feature-led instead of outcome-led
- Ends with punctuation (!, ?, ...)

**Your feedback template:**
> "Your subject line is: '[their subject]'. Here's the problem: [diagnosis]. Here's the fix: [rewrite it on the call]. That change alone should get you +10–20% more opens."

**Example:**
> "Your subject 'Introducing DevTool X' — that reads like a product announcement. Founders delete those instantly. Try: 'Cut deploy time by 40%.' Same product, different frame. Opens will jump."

---

#### Fix 2: Email Body Structure
**Most common issues:**
- Too long (>120 words)
- No personalization / sounds like a template
- Buried CTA (ask is on line 12 after 3 paragraphs)
- Talking about features instead of outcomes
- No credibility signal (social proof, case study)

**Your feedback template:**
> "Your email is [X] words. That's too long — B2B founders skim, not read. Here's what you cut: [specific lines]. Move your CTA from line [X] to line 3. Add one proof point: '[example]'. Now it's tight, credible, and scannable."

**Example:**
> "You have 4 paragraphs explaining how the product works. That's documentation, not a cold email. Cut paragraphs 2–3. Replace them with one line: 'We helped [Company X] cut onboarding time from 5 days to 2 hours.' That's your credibility. Then ask for 15 minutes. Done."

---

#### Fix 3: Hook / Personalization
**Most common issues:**
- Generic opening ("Hope you're doing well")
- No specific reference to their company, product, or recent event
- Hook doesn't tie to a pain point

**Your feedback template:**
> "Your opening line is generic — '[their line]'. You need a hook that's specific to *them*. Did they recently raise funding? Launch on Product Hunt? Post about a challenge on LinkedIn? Use that. Example: '[rewrite with specific hook].' Now they know this isn't a blast."

**Example:**
> "You open with 'I wanted to reach out...' — that's filler. Start with: 'I saw you're hiring 3 SDRs this quarter — that means cold email volume is about to 5x.' Now they're listening, because that's their reality."

---

### Transition to Paid Session (3 minutes)

After delivering the 3 fixes:

**You say:**
> "Okay, so those are 3 things you can fix today. If you rewrite your email with those changes, you should see response rates go from [current estimate, e.g. 5%] to closer to [target, e.g. 12–15%]."

**Pause.** Let them absorb. They'll usually say "thanks" or ask a question. Then:

> "Now, that's the surface-level stuff. If you want to go deeper — full sequence strategy, follow-up timing, testing plan, scaling tactics — I do a 90-minute paid session where we build out your entire cold email system. It's $200. We'd walk out with a 5-email sequence, a list of 100 prospects, and a week-by-week execution plan. Interested?"

**If yes:**
> "Great. Here's my calendar link: [insert link]. Grab a slot this week or next, and send me 2–3 of your best cold emails beforehand so I can prep. We'll make it count."

**If no / hesitation:**
> "No problem. Take these 3 fixes and run with them. If you want the deeper session later, just reply to my email and we'll set it up."

**Don't oversell.** If they're not ready, let them go. The free teardown already proved value — they'll come back if it works.

## Call Scripts for Common Objections

### "Can I think about it?"
**You say:**
> "Of course. One thing to note: cold email compounds. Every week you wait is 50–100 emails that could've been better. But no pressure — grab a slot when you're ready."

### "Can you send me a proposal?"
**You say:**
> "It's pretty straightforward: $200, 90 minutes, you walk away with a full sequence + execution plan. If you want it, just book the slot and we're good to go. If not, no worries."

### "I need to check with my co-founder / team."
**You say:**
> "Makes sense. Loop them in, and if they want to join the 90-minute session, that's fine — no extra charge. Just book when you're aligned."

### "I can't afford $200 right now."
**You say (if you believe they're a good fit and will get value):**
> "Got it. Tell you what: if you can't swing $200 today, let's do the 90-minute session for $100 and we'll focus on just your first email + follow-up sequence. Still actionable, just narrower scope. Work for you?"

**OR (if they're clearly not serious):**
> "Understood. Stick with the 3 fixes I just gave you and you'll still see improvement. Good luck!"

## Post-Call Actions

### If they booked:
1. Send calendar confirmation email immediately
2. Ask them to send 2–3 cold emails before the session
3. Prep: review their emails, research their ICP, draft sequence outline
4. After paid session: send deliverable (sequence doc) within 24 hours
5. Add entry to `revenue-manual.json`:
   ```json
   {"date": "2026-05-01", "revenue_usd": 200, "notes": "Consulting session with [Name] from [Company]"}
   ```

### If they didn't book:
1. Send follow-up email within 1 hour:
   > "Hey [Name], quick recap of the 3 fixes we covered: [list them]. Let me know if you want to go deeper — calendar link is here: [link]. Otherwise, good luck with the rewrite!"
2. Add to CRM or tracking sheet: "Teardown delivered, no conversion yet"
3. Follow up in 7 days if no reply: "How'd the rewrite go?"

---

## Key Success Metrics

**Track these per call:**
- Did they say the teardown was valuable? (qualitative)
- Did they book the paid session? (conversion)
- If no: what was the objection? (iterate script)

**Target benchmarks:**
- Free teardown → paid session conversion: **30–40%**
- If you're below 20%, the teardown isn't demonstrating enough value — go deeper on the fixes or add a 4th insight.

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Don't run over 15 minutes** — you offered 15, stick to it. Running long devalues the paid session.  
❌ **Don't give away the full strategy for free** — 3 fixes is generous but leaves room for more.  
❌ **Don't pressure** — if they're not ready, let them walk. Desperation kills conversions.  
❌ **Don't trash their current emails** — frame every critique as "here's a better way" not "this is bad."  
❌ **Don't forget to track revenue** — update `revenue-manual.json` after every paid session.
