# Talking Points — Free 15-Minute Teardown Call

## Pre-Call Prep (5 min before call)
- Read their cold email sequence (they should have sent it before the call)
- Look at their website: what's their product? Who's their ICP?
- Check their LinkedIn: any recent posts about sales/outreach struggles?
- Identify the 3 biggest issues in their sequence (use checklist below)

---

## Call Structure (15 minutes max)

### Intro (1 min)
"Hey [Name], thanks for hopping on. I've already looked at your sequence. I have 3 specific things I think you should fix — I'll walk through them quickly, you can ask questions, and then you decide if you want to go deeper with a paid session. Sound good?"

### Issue #1 — Subject Line (3 min)
**Common problems:**
- Too generic ("Quick question" / "Intro")
- Too salesy ("Increase your revenue by 50%")
- Too long (>6 words)
- No curiosity hook

**Good subject lines:**
- Reference something specific about them: "Your [feature] launch"
- Create curiosity: "Why your [metric] is stuck"
- Offer value: "Quick audit of your [thing they care about]"

**Advice:** "Your subject line is `[their subject]`. The problem is `[why it's not working]`. Try this instead: `[rewritten subject]`. That's more likely to get opened because `[reason]`."

### Issue #2 — Opening Line (4 min)
**Common problems:**
- Generic flattery ("Love your product!")
- Talking about themselves first ("I help companies...")
- No specific pain point mentioned
- Too long before getting to the point

**Good opening lines:**
- Reference a trigger event: "Saw you just raised a seed round..."
- Call out a problem: "Most [their role] I talk to struggle with [pain point]..."
- Pattern interrupt: "I'm going to guess your [metric] is below [number]..."

**Advice:** "Your opening is `[quote their line]`. The issue is it doesn't hook them. They don't know why they should care yet. Try: `[rewritten opening]`. That addresses a real pain point they have right now."

### Issue #3 — CTA or Timing (3 min)
**Common problems:**
- Asking for too much commitment ("30-min call?")
- Weak CTA ("Let me know if interested")
- Sending follow-ups too fast or too slow
- No value in the follow-up emails

**Good CTAs:**
- Low friction: "Worth a 15-min chat?"
- Time-bound: "Have 10 min this week?"
- Direct: "Should I send you the case study?"

**Advice:** "Your CTA asks for `[their ask]`. That's too big a leap. Try: `[rewritten CTA]`. And your follow-up timing: `[adjust if needed]`. Most people need 3 touches before they respond."

### Wrap-Up (4 min)
"So those are the 3 things I'd fix first: `[summary]`. You can implement those today and probably see a bump in response rate within a week."

**If they're engaged:** "If you want to go deeper — full sequence rewrite, ICP strategy, testing plan — I do a 90-minute paid session for $200. We'd draft a complete 5-email sequence you can use immediately. Interested?"

**If they're hesitant:** "No pressure. Take these fixes, try them out. If you want to revisit later, just email me. I'm [your email]."

**If they say yes to paid session:** "Great. I'll send you a payment link and a calendar invite right after this call. Once you pay, you book the session. We'll knock it out within the next week or two."

## Issue Identification Checklist

Before the call, score their sequence on these 7 dimensions (1–5, 1=broken, 5=great):

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Subject line (specific, curiosity, <6 words) | | |
| Opening line (hooks with pain point or trigger event) | | |
| Value prop (clear, specific, addresses their problem) | | |
| Social proof (case study, metric, credible signal) | | |
| CTA (low friction, clear next step) | | |
| Email length (150–200 words max) | | |
| Follow-up strategy (3 touches, adds value each time) | | |

Pick the 3 lowest-scoring dimensions and fix those on the call.

## Objection Handling

**"I'm already getting decent response rates."**  
→ "What's your current rate? If it's above 15%, you're doing great and probably don't need my help. Most people I talk to are stuck at <5%."

**"I can't afford $200 right now."**  
→ "Totally fair. Take the free fixes, implement them, see if they work. If you hit a wall later, I'm here."

**"I've already tried everything."**  
→ "Walk me through what you've tested. I bet there's something you haven't tried yet — most people over-optimize the wrong parts."

**"I need to think about it."**  
→ "No problem. You have my email. The offer stands. Good luck with the changes."

## Post-Call Actions

**If they book the paid session:**
- Send Stripe/PayPal/Venmo payment link immediately
- Send calendar link for 90-min session
- Ask them to send any additional materials before the session (current analytics, competitor emails, etc.)

**If they don't book:**
- Send a follow-up email 1 week later: "Hey [Name], curious — did you implement those 3 fixes? How'd it go?"
- If no response, wait 2 weeks and send a case study update: "Thought you'd like to see this: helped [Founder X] improve reply rate from 4% to 17% using the same framework we talked about."

**Always:**
- Record the outcome in `task-log.md` or `experiments.md`
- If they paid, record revenue in `revenue-manual.json` immediately
- If they had a specific objection or question, flag it for the agent to refine the pitch
