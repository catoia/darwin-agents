# Strategy: Budget Recipe Blog

## The Opportunity
Millions of people search for "budget recipes," "cheap meals," and "meal prep under $20" every month. Most results are ad-heavy listicles or paywalled content. There's room for a clean, well-optimized site with actionable recipes targeting the long tail: "cheap dinner ideas for 2," "budget chicken recipes," etc.

## Monetization Path
1. **AdSense** — display ads on recipe pages (revenue starts day 1 after approval)
2. **Amazon Associates** — affiliate links to budget kitchen tools, bulk ingredients, cookbooks
3. **Sponsored content** — once traffic hits 10k/month, pitch budget food brands for sponsored posts

## What the Agent Does
- Write SEO-optimized recipe content targeting long-tail keywords
- Deploy pages to Cloudflare Pages
- Monitor traffic and optimize top performers
- Submit to Google Search Console, optimize for featured snippets
- Run A/B tests on CTAs and affiliate placement

## What the Human Must Do
- Apply for Google AdSense account (agent provides copy/screenshots)
- Set up Amazon Associates account and provide API credentials
- Approve any paid tool/service requests via human_task

## First 3 Steps to $1
1. **Week 1:** Publish 10 budget recipe pages targeting long-tail keywords ("cheap pasta dishes for students," "budget meal prep chicken," etc.). Deploy. Submit sitemap to Google.
2. **Week 2:** Add Amazon affiliate links to kitchen tools mentioned in recipes (cutting boards, measuring cups, bulk spice containers). Apply for AdSense.
3. **Week 3:** Monitor Search Console for indexing. Rewrite or expand the top 3 pages based on early clicks. Push for first affiliate commission or AdSense approval.

## Revenue Target
**$200 in 6 weeks** from combined AdSense + Amazon affiliate earnings.
