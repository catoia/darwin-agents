Budget Meal Prep Chicken: A Week of Meals Under $18

Cost: $17.50 total | Prep time: 2 hours | Makes 7 meals

One shopping trip. One Sunday afternoon. Seven chicken meals ready to go. This meal prep strategy uses 3 lbs of chicken prepared three different ways to keep you fed all week without getting bored.

The Master Shopping List

Protein ($9.50)

  • 3 lbs chicken breast or thighs ($9.50) - split into three 1-lb portions

Carbs ($3.00)

  • 3 lbs rice or quinoa ($2.00)
  • 1 lb pasta ($1.00)

Vegetables ($3.50)

  • 2 bags frozen broccoli ($3.00)
  • 1 bag baby carrots ($1.50)
  • 1 onion ($0.50)

Sauces & Seasonings ($1.50)

  • Soy sauce, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, BBQ sauce

The 3-Way Chicken Strategy

Cook your 3 lbs of chicken three different ways to create variety:

Batch 1: Teriyaki Chicken (1 lb) → 3 Meals

  1. Cut 1 lb chicken into cubes.
  2. Make teriyaki sauce: 1/4 cup soy sauce + 2 tbsp honey + 1 tsp garlic + 1 tsp ginger.
  3. Pan-fry chicken, add sauce, simmer until sticky.
  4. Divide into 3 glass containers with rice and broccoli.

Batch 2: Italian Herb Chicken (1 lb) → 2 Meals

  1. Season 1 lb chicken with Italian herbs, garlic powder, salt, pepper.
  2. Bake at 400°F for 25 minutes or until 165°F internal temp.
  3. Slice and serve over pasta with marinara sauce.
  4. Makes 2 large portions.

Batch 3: BBQ Chicken (1 lb) → 2 Meals

  1. Season 1 lb chicken with paprika, garlic powder, salt.
  2. Grill or bake until almost done.
  3. Brush with BBQ sauce, cook 5 more minutes.
  4. Serve with rice and roasted carrots.

Your 2-Hour Prep Timeline

Step-by-Step

  1. 0:00 - Preheat oven to 400°F. Start cooking all the rice (7 cups cooked).
  2. 0:10 - While rice cooks, cut and season all chicken into 3 batches.
  3. 0:20 - Put Italian herb chicken in oven (25 min).
  4. 0:25 - Start cooking teriyaki chicken on stovetop.
  5. 0:35 - Teriyaki done. Start BBQ chicken.
  6. 0:45 - Italian chicken done. BBQ chicken cooking.
  7. 0:50 - Steam all frozen broccoli in microwave.
  8. 1:00 - All cooking done. Let everything cool 15 minutes.
  9. 1:15 - Assemble containers: protein + carb + vegetable.
  10. 1:45 - Label containers with date and meal type.
  11. 2:00 - Done! Refrigerate all containers.

Storage & Safety Tips

  • Cooked chicken lasts 4 days in fridge—freeze anything you won't eat by Thursday
  • Cool chicken completely before sealing containers
  • Reheat to 165°F before eating
  • Add a splash of water when reheating to prevent dryness
  • Store sauces separately if possible for better texture

How to Scale This Strategy

For 2 people: Double the recipe, costs $35 for 14 meals

For just dinners (not lunch): Cut recipe in half, costs $9 for 3-4 dinners

Add variety: Substitute 1 lb chicken with ground turkey or tofu
