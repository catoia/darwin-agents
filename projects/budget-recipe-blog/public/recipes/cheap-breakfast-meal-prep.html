Option 1: Breakfast Burrito Meal Prep

Cost: $11.20 for 5 burritos | Freezer-friendly!

Shopping List

  • 10 eggs ($3.00)
  • 1 lb breakfast sausage or bacon ($3.50)
  • 5 large flour tortillas ($1.50)
  • 1.5 cups shredded cheese ($1.80)
  • 1 bell pepper ($1.00)
  • 1 onion ($0.50)
  • Salt, pepper, hot sauce

Prep Instructions

  1. Cook sausage or bacon until done. Chop into pieces.
  2. Sauté diced peppers and onions until soft.
  3. Scramble all 10 eggs in the same pan.
  4. Mix eggs, meat, and vegetables together.
  5. Warm tortillas for 10 seconds in microwave (makes them easier to roll).
  6. Divide mixture among 5 tortillas. Top each with cheese.
  7. Roll tightly into burritos.
  8. Wrap each individually in foil.
  9. Label with date and store in freezer.

To reheat: Unwrap foil, wrap in damp paper towel, microwave 2-3 minutes from frozen (or 1-2 minutes if thawed).

Option 2: Overnight Oats (5 servings)

Cost: $6.50 for 5 breakfasts | No cooking required!

Shopping List

  • 2.5 cups rolled oats ($1.50)
  • 2.5 cups milk ($1.80)
  • 2 bananas ($1.00)
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter ($0.80)
  • 2 tbsp honey ($0.60)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract ($0.30)
  • Optional: cinnamon, berries ($0.50)

Prep Instructions

  1. Get 5 mason jars or containers with lids.
  2. In each jar, add:
    • 1/2 cup oats
    • 1/2 cup milk
    • 1/2 sliced banana
    • 1 tbsp peanut butter
    • Drizzle of honey
    • Dash of vanilla and cinnamon
  3. Stir well in each jar.
  4. Seal lids and refrigerate.
  5. Grab and go in the morning! (Can eat cold or microwave 1 minute)

Option 3: Egg Muffin Cups (12 muffins = 5-6 breakfasts)

Cost: $9.80 for 12 muffins | High protein, low carb!

Shopping List

  • 12 eggs ($3.60)
  • 1 cup diced ham or cooked sausage ($2.50)
  • 1 cup shredded cheese ($1.20)
  • 1 bell pepper, diced ($1.00)
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped ($1.00)
  • 1/4 cup milk ($0.20)
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder ($0.30)

Prep Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Spray a 12-cup non-stick muffin tin with cooking spray.
  2. Whisk together all 12 eggs, milk, salt, pepper, garlic powder.
  3. Divide ham, cheese, peppers, and spinach evenly among muffin cups.
  4. Pour egg mixture over the fillings, about 3/4 full.
  5. Bake 20-25 minutes until eggs are set and tops are golden.
  6. Let cool completely.
  7. Store in fridge up to 5 days, or freeze for longer.

To reheat: Microwave 2 muffins for 60-90 seconds.

Option 4: Make-Ahead Pancake Mix

Cost: $8.00 for 20 pancakes | Just add water!

Homemade Pancake Mix (makes 20 pancakes)

  • 3 cups flour ($0.90)
  • 3 tbsp sugar ($0.20)
  • 3 tbsp baking powder ($0.60)
  • 1 tsp salt ($0.05)
  • For cooking: milk, eggs, butter (about $3.00 total)
  • Syrup for serving ($1.50)

Strategy

  1. Mix all dry ingredients in a large container. Store in pantry.
  2. Sunday night: make a batch of 20 pancakes (takes 30 minutes).
  3. Let cool completely.
  4. Stack with parchment paper between each pancake.
  5. Store in fridge (5 days) or freezer (3 months).
  6. Reheat 4 pancakes in toaster or microwave for 60 seconds.

Tips for Breakfast Meal Prep Success

  • Prep on Sunday: One hour sets you up for the whole week
  • Label everything: Write the date and contents
  • Keep it simple: Start with one recipe you know you'll eat
