1. Classic Beef Tacos ($12.50, serves 6)

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs ground beef (80/20) ($6.00)
  • 12 taco shells or soft tortillas ($2.50)
  • Taco seasoning ($0.80)
  • Shredded lettuce ($1.50)
  • 2 tomatoes, diced ($1.00)
  • 2 cups shredded cheese ($2.40)
  • Sour cream ($1.30)

Instructions

  1. Brown ground beef in a cast iron skillet, breaking it up with a ground meat chopper as it cooks.
  2. Drain excess fat.
  3. Add taco seasoning and 1/2 cup water. Simmer 5 minutes.
  4. Warm taco shells according to package.
  5. Set up a taco bar with all toppings.

2. Spaghetti with Meat Sauce ($9.80, serves 6)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef ($4.00)
  • 1 lb spaghetti ($1.20)
  • 1 large jar marinara sauce ($2.50)
  • 1 onion, diced ($0.50)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced ($0.30)
  • Italian seasoning ($0.30)
  • Parmesan for serving ($1.00)

Instructions

  1. Cook spaghetti according to package directions.
  2. Meanwhile, brown beef with onion and garlic.
  3. Drain fat, add marinara sauce and Italian seasoning.
  4. Simmer 15 minutes.
  5. Serve over spaghetti with Parmesan.

3. Beef and Bean Chili ($13.50, serves 8)

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs ground beef ($6.00)
  • 2 cans kidney beans ($1.80)
  • 2 cans diced tomatoes ($2.40)
  • 1 onion ($0.50)
  • 1 bell pepper ($1.00)
  • Chili powder, cumin, garlic powder ($0.80)
  • Optional toppings: cheese, sour cream ($1.00)

Instructions

  1. Brown beef with diced onion and bell pepper.
  2. Add beans (drained), tomatoes (with juice), and spices.
  3. Bring to boil, then simmer 30 minutes.
  4. Serve with toppings and cornbread if desired.

Pro tip: This freezes perfectly. Make a double batch!

4. Stuffed Bell Peppers ($14.20, serves 4)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef ($4.00)
  • 4 large bell peppers ($4.00)
  • 1 cup cooked rice ($0.50)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes ($1.20)
  • 1.5 cups shredded cheese ($1.80)
  • 1 onion ($0.50)
  • Italian seasoning, garlic powder ($0.40)
  • 1 cup beef broth ($0.80)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Cut tops off peppers, remove seeds. Place in baking dish.
  3. Brown beef with onion, drain fat.
  4. Mix beef with rice, half the tomatoes, 1 cup cheese, and seasonings.
  5. Stuff peppers with mixture.
  6. Pour remaining tomatoes and broth around peppers.
  7. Cover with foil, bake 35 minutes.
  8. Uncover, top with remaining cheese, bake 10 more minutes.

5. Beef Fried Rice ($11.00, serves 4)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef ($4.00)
  • 4 cups cooked rice, cold ($1.20)
  • 2 cups frozen peas and carrots ($1.60)
  • 3 eggs ($0.90)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce ($0.50)
  • 4 green onions ($0.80)
  • 3 cloves garlic ($0.20)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil ($0.30)
  • Optional: sesame oil ($0.50)

Instructions

  1. Brown ground beef in a cast iron skillet or wok. Remove and set aside.
  2. In same pan, scramble eggs. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add oil, garlic, and frozen vegetables. Stir-fry 3 minutes.
  4. Add cold rice, breaking up clumps. Stir-fry 5 minutes.
  5. Return beef and eggs to pan. Add soy sauce.
  6. Toss everything together. Garnish with green onions.

How to Buy Ground Beef on a Budget

  • 80/20 is the sweet spot: Cheaper than lean, still not too greasy
  • Buy in bulk: 5 lb chubs are often $1-2/lb cheaper
  • Divide and freeze: Portion into 1 lb bags before freezing
  • Watch for sales: Stock up when it's under $3/lb
  • Stretch it: Add beans, rice, or vegetables to make beef go further
  • Drain the fat: You're literally pouring money down the drain, but it makes dishes healthier
