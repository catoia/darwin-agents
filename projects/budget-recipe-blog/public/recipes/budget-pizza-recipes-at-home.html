Budget Pizza Recipes at Home

Cost: $4-8 for whole pizza (feeds 4) | Prep: 15 min + 1 hour rise | Cook: 12-15 min

Pizza delivery runs $20-35 for a family. A homemade pizza of the same quality costs $4-8 and is ready in under 2 hours (mostly hands-off rising time). The dough recipe below is bulletproof, and the toppings are where you get creative with what you have in the fridge.

The Budget Pizza Dough (Master Recipe)

Cost: ~$0.80 | Makes 2 medium pizzas or 1 large

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour ($0.30)
  • 1 packet (2.25 tsp) instant yeast ($0.25)
  • 1 tsp salt ($0.02)
  • 1 tsp sugar ($0.02)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil ($0.20)
  • 1 cup warm water (110°F — not hot, not cold) ($0)

Instructions

  1. Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Wait 5 minutes until foamy (proves yeast is alive).
  2. Add olive oil, salt, and flour. Mix until dough forms.
  3. Knead on floured surface 8 minutes until smooth and elastic, OR use a stand mixer with dough hook for 5 minutes.
  4. Place in oiled bowl, cover with damp towel. Rise in warm spot 1 hour until doubled.
  5. Punch down. Stretch or roll out to desired thickness.
  6. No-rise shortcut: Skip the rise, stretch out immediately — the pizza will be denser but still good. Saves 1 hour.

1. Classic Margherita Pizza

Total cost: $5.80 | The original

Toppings for 1 pizza

  • Budget pizza dough ($0.80)
  • 1/2 cup crushed tomatoes or pizza sauce ($0.60)
  • 1.5 cups shredded mozzarella ($1.80)
  • Fresh basil leaves ($0.60)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil ($0.40)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced ($0.15)
  • Salt, red pepper flakes
  • Pinch of sugar for sauce ($0.02)
  • Cornmeal for the pan ($0.08)
  • Parmesan to finish ($0.35)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to highest setting (500°F) with a baking sheet inside. Hot pan = crispy crust.
  2. Season crushed tomatoes with garlic, pinch of sugar, salt, and red pepper flakes.
  3. Stretch dough on parchment paper dusted with cornmeal.
  4. Spread sauce, leaving 1-inch border. Top with mozzarella.
  5. Carefully slide pizza onto hot pan. Bake 10-12 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
  6. Drizzle with olive oil. Add fresh basil and parmesan immediately after baking.
Nutrition (per slice, 8 slices): ~250 calories | 11g protein | 32g carbs | 9g fat

2. White Garlic Pizza

Cost: $6.20 | No tomato sauce — just garlicky cream

Toppings

  • Budget pizza dough ($0.80)
  • 3 tbsp butter, 4 cloves garlic ($0.45)
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese ($1.80)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella ($1.20)
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen spinach ($0.80)
  • Dried Italian herbs, red pepper flakes, salt ($0.20)
  • Parmesan to finish ($0.35)
  • Lemon zest optional ($0.20)
  • Olive oil ($0.42)

Instructions

  1. Melt butter, add garlic, cook 2 minutes. This is your "sauce."
  2. Spread garlic butter on stretched dough. Dollop ricotta over it.
  3. Squeeze all water from spinach, scatter over pizza. Top with mozzarella.
  4. Sprinkle with herbs and red pepper flakes.
  5. Bake at 500°F for 10-12 minutes. Finish with parmesan and lemon zest.

3. Budget Meat Lovers Pizza

Cost: $7.80 | For carnivores on a budget

Toppings

  • Budget pizza dough ($0.80)
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce ($0.60)
  • 1 cup shredded mozzarella ($1.20)
  • 2 oz pepperoni ($1.50)
  • 2 oz Italian sausage or ground beef, browned ($1.20)
  • 2 strips bacon, cooked and crumbled ($0.80)
  • 1/4 cup diced bell pepper and onion ($0.50)
  • Red pepper flakes, Italian seasoning
  • Extra cheese ($0.40)
  • Garlic powder ($0.10)
  • Olive oil ($0.20)
  • Parmesan ($0.50)

Instructions

  1. Brown sausage/ground beef. Drain.
  2. Spread sauce on stretched dough. Season sauce with garlic powder and Italian seasoning.
  3. Add half the cheese as a base layer.
  4. Layer all meats and vegetables.
  5. Top with remaining cheese. Drizzle olive oil on edges for golden crust.
  6. Bake 12-15 minutes at 500°F.

Quick No-Dough Options

  • 4. Tortilla Pizza: Large flour tortilla as base. Broil 3 minutes to crisp. Add sauce, cheese, toppings. Broil 2-3 more minutes. Ultra-fast, $2-3.
  • 5. English Muffin Pizzas: Perfect for kids, customizable. Toast muffins, add sauce and cheese, broil 3 minutes. $3 for 4 people.
  • 6. Naan Pizza: Naan flatbread as pizza base. No rising needed. Great for single servings. Add whatever toppings. 10 minutes, $3 each.

Budget Pizza Topping Ideas

Use whatever is cheap or already in your fridge:

  • Vegetables: Frozen spinach (thaw, squeeze), canned mushrooms, canned corn, sliced onion, frozen peppers
  • Proteins: Canned tuna, leftover chicken, crumbled breakfast sausage, scrambled eggs (breakfast pizza!)
  • Cheese substitutions: Cottage cheese blended smooth works as a ricotta substitute; any melting cheese works
  • Sauce alternatives: Crushed canned tomatoes + garlic + salt + olive oil; pesto (basil + oil + parmesan); leftover pasta sauce

  • Large Baking Sheet - Works great for sheet-pan pizza ($25)
  • KitchenAid Stand Mixer - Make dough hands-free ($299, worth it for families)
