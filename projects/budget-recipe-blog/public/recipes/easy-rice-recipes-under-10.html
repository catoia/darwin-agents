Easy Rice Recipes Under $10

Cost: $4-10 for 4 servings | Prep: 5-10 min | Cook: 20-30 min

Rice is one of the cheapest foods on earth — a 5-pound bag costs under $5 and feeds a family for weeks. The trick is turning it from plain starch into a full, satisfying meal. These 8 easy rice recipes under $10 show you exactly how.

1. Coconut Rice and Black Beans

Cost: $7.50 | 25 minutes | Tropical vibes on a budget

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 1.5 cups white rice ($0.60)
  • 1 can coconut milk ($1.80)
  • 1 cup water ($0)
  • 2 cans black beans ($1.80)
  • 1 lime ($0.40)
  • 1 tsp cumin ($0.10)
  • 1 onion, 3 garlic cloves ($0.70)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil ($0.20)
  • Salt, pepper, chili powder
  • Cilantro optional ($0.50)
  • Plantains optional ($1.40)

Instructions

  1. Cook rice in the coconut milk + water instead of plain water. Bring to boil, cover, simmer 18 min.
  2. While rice cooks, sauté onion and garlic in olive oil 5 minutes.
  3. Add drained beans, cumin, chili powder, salt. Cook 10 minutes until beans are flavored.
  4. Squeeze lime over beans. Serve over coconut rice.
  5. Top with cilantro. Fry sliced plantains in a bit of oil for a side if budget allows.
Nutrition (per serving): ~450 calories | 16g protein | 72g carbs | 12g fat

2. Chicken Fried Rice

Cost: $8.50 | 15 minutes — best with day-old rice

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 3 cups cooked cold rice ($0.60)
  • 1/2 lb chicken breast or thigh, diced ($2.50)
  • 3 eggs ($0.60)
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots ($0.80)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce ($0.30)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce optional ($0.30)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil ($0.20)
  • 3 cloves garlic, 1 tsp ginger ($0.30)
  • 3 green onions ($0.50)
  • Sesame oil ($0.30) [AFFILIATE_PLACEHOLDER]
  • White pepper (makes a difference!) ($0.30)

Instructions

  1. Heat wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking. Add 1 tbsp oil.
  2. Cook chicken until golden, about 4 minutes. Set aside.
  3. Add more oil. Stir-fry frozen vegetables 2 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and ginger, 30 seconds. Push everything to the sides.
  5. Scramble eggs in the center until just set, then mix everything together.
  6. Add cold rice. Stir-fry 3 minutes, pressing rice into the wok to get crispy bits.
  7. Return chicken. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, white pepper, sesame oil.
  8. Toss, top with green onions.

3. Spanish Rice with Sausage

Cost: $9.20 | One pan, 30 minutes

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 1/2 lb smoked sausage, sliced ($2.80)
  • 1.5 cups long-grain rice ($0.60)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes ($0.90)
  • 2.5 cups chicken broth ($1.20)
  • 1 onion, 1 bell pepper ($1.20)
  • 3 cloves garlic ($0.20)
  • 1 tsp cumin, smoked paprika, oregano ($0.20)
  • Salt, pepper, hot sauce
  • 1 tbsp olive oil ($0.20)
  • Frozen peas optional ($0.90)

Instructions

  1. Brown sausage in olive oil over medium-high heat. Remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté onion and bell pepper in same pan, 5 minutes.
  3. Add garlic and spices, cook 1 minute.
  4. Add uncooked rice, stir to coat in the oil and spices, 2 minutes.
  5. Add tomatoes and broth. Return sausage. Bring to boil.
  6. Cover tightly, reduce heat to low, cook 18 minutes. Add peas in last 3 minutes.
  7. Fluff with fork, let rest 5 minutes before serving.

4. Greek Lemon Rice (Avgolemono Style)

Cost: $5.80 | Elegant, simple, surprisingly filling

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 1.5 cups rice ($0.60)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth ($1.60)
  • 2 eggs ($0.40)
  • 2 lemons, juiced ($0.80)
  • 2 tbsp butter ($0.20)
  • Salt, white pepper
  • Fresh dill or parsley ($0.50)
  • Side bread ($1.00)
  • Feta crumbles optional ($0.70)

Instructions

  1. Cook rice in the broth instead of water (adds huge flavor at zero extra cost).
  2. When rice is done, reduce heat to lowest setting.
  3. Whisk eggs and lemon juice together vigorously.
  4. Take a ladle of hot rice liquid, slowly whisk it into the egg mixture to temper.
  5. Pour tempered egg mixture back into the rice, stir constantly for 2 minutes.
  6. The rice becomes creamy and silky. Add butter, dill, salt, white pepper.
  7. Serve with bread and feta crumbles if using.

More Budget Rice Ideas

  • 5. Kimchi Fried Rice: Leftover rice + kimchi ($2/jar) + eggs + soy sauce. 10-minute flavor bomb. ~$4 for two.
  • 6. Arroz con Leche (Sweet Rice Pudding): Rice + milk + sugar + cinnamon. Dessert or breakfast, under $3.
  • 7. Vegetable Rice Pilaf: Toast rice in butter, add broth + frozen vegetables, bake at 350°F 25 minutes. Hands-off side dish, $4.
  • 8. Teriyaki Rice Bowls: Rice + sautéed whatever-vegetables-you-have + homemade teriyaki (soy sauce, honey, garlic, ginger). $5.

The Right Way to Cook Perfect Rice

  • Rinse rice until water runs clear — removes excess starch, prevents stickiness
  • Ratio: 1 cup rice to 2 cups water (for white long-grain)
  • Bring to boil, then immediately cover and drop to lowest heat
  • Never lift the lid during cooking — steam is doing the work
  • Rest 5 minutes off heat, then fluff with a fork (not a spoon)
