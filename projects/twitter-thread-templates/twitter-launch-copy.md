## How to post the free samples

**Option A: Reply thread (recommended for engagement)**
- Post the hook tweet
- Reply to yourself with each template summary
- Each template gets 1-2 tweets max
- Focus on the "When to use" and "Why it works" sections
- End with CTA to full pack

**Option B: Google Doc + link**
- Post the hook tweet
- Link to a Google Doc with all 5 templates in full
- Requires email gate if you want to capture leads
- Lower friction, higher conversion

**Option C: Tweet thread with examples**
- Post the hook tweet  
- For each template, show a 3-tweet EXAMPLE of it in action
- Then offer the full template
- Most engaging but takes more time

## Template summaries for reply thread

If you're doing a reply thread, here's the abbreviated version of each template:

**Template 1: Problem-Agitate-Solution**
→ When to use: Positioning your product/insight as the solution
→ Structure: Name the problem (1) → Make pain real (2-3) → Your solution (4-6) → Proof (7) → CTA (8)
→ Works because: People buy when pain > friction

**Template 2: Build Story**  
→ When to use: You just shipped something and want to share learnings
→ Structure: Hook with outcome (1) → Why you built it (2-3) → The surprise (4-5) → Key lessons (6-7) → Results (8) → What's next (9)
→ Works because: Founders learn best from other founders' real experiences

**Template 3: Unpopular Opinion**
→ When to use: Challenging conventional wisdom to spark debate
→ Structure: State opinion (1) → Acknowledge common belief (2) → Why it's wrong (3-5) → Data/evidence (6) → Alternative approach (7) → Invite discussion (8)
→ Works because: Contrarian takes create engagement (people HAVE to respond)

**Template 4: Data-Driven**
→ When to use: You have surprising data or research
→ Structure: Lead with shocking stat (1) → Context (2) → 3 key findings (3-6) → Implications (7) → What doesn't work (8) → CTA (9)
→ Works because: Specific numbers = credibility. Surprising data = shares.

**Template 5: Case Study**
→ When to use: You have a customer success story or transformation
→ Structure: The transformation (1) → Before state (2-3) → The solution (4-7) → After results (8) → Key lesson (9) → CTA (10)
→ Works because: Proof > promises. Specific examples beat general advice.

## Tracking engagement

After posting, track:
- Likes (passive interest)
- Replies (active engagement - MOST VALUABLE)
- Retweets (distribution signal)
- Bookmarks (intent to use later)
- Profile visits (consideration)
- Link clicks (if you include a landing page)

**Success threshold:** 50+ combined engagement within 24 hours = validation to build full product

**Decision tree:**
- 50+ engagement → build all 30 templates + launch on Gumroad
- 20-49 engagement → investigate what resonated, iterate templates, repost improved version
- <20 engagement → pivot (different audience, different format, or kill project)

---

## Timing strategy

**Best time to post (B2B SaaS audience):**
- Weekday mornings: 7-9am ET (founders scrolling with coffee)
- Lunch: 12-1pm ET (quick Twitter break)
- Evening: 6-8pm ET (winding down, longer browsing sessions)

**Avoid:**
- Weekends (lower B2B engagement)
- Monday mornings (people catching up on work, not Twitter)
- Late night (thread will be buried by morning)

**Recommendation:** Post Tuesday-Thursday, 8am ET for maximum reach

## Reply strategy (human execution)

When people engage:
- Reply to every person who comments in first 2 hours (algorithm boost)
- Ask questions: "Which template will you try first?"
- Offer help: "Need help filling one out? DM me your topic"
- Collect interest: Keep a list of everyone who says "interested" for launch day

When people DM:
- Respond within 1 hour if possible
- Offer to help them write their first thread using a template
- This builds goodwill + gives you examples for the paid product
