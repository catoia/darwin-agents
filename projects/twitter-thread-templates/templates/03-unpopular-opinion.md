# Unpopular Opinion Thread Template

**When to use:** When you want to challenge conventional wisdom in your industry. High engagement potential because contrarian takes spark debate. Use carefully — you need to defend your position with evidence or experience.

---

## The Thread Structure

**[1/8] Hook - State the unpopular opinion (be bold, specific)**

Unpopular opinion:

[Your contrarian take]

Here's why everyone gets this wrong →


**[2/8] Acknowledge the conventional wisdom**

Most [industry/role] believe:

"[Popular belief in quotes]"

I get why this sounds right. [Charitable interpretation of why people believe this]


**[3/8] Explain why it's wrong (set up your argument)**

But here's the problem:

[Why conventional wisdom fails in practice]

I learned this the hard way. [Brief personal experience]


**[4/8] Your counter-argument (point 1)**

First: [Counter-argument point 1]

Example: [Specific evidence or story]

[Why this matters]


**[5/8] Your counter-argument (point 2)**

Second: [Counter-argument point 2]

Think about it: [Logic that supports your position]

[Common objection addressed]


**[6/8] The data/evidence (if you have it)**

The data backs this up:

• [Stat/example 1]
• [Stat/example 2]
• [Stat/example 3]

[Interpretation of what this means]


**[7/8] What to do instead (your alternative approach)**

So what should you do instead?

[Your recommended approach]

[Why this is better / how it's different]


**[8/8] CTA - invite discussion / offer deeper dive**

Disagree? Tell me why. I'm genuinely curious.

Agree? Here's how to implement this: [link/resource]

Or follow for more takes that'll make you question everything → [handle]

## Example (B2B SaaS founder context)

**[1/8]** Unpopular opinion:

"Product-market fit" is a useless concept that causes more harm than good.

Here's why everyone gets this wrong →

**[2/8]** Most founders believe:

"Once you find product-market fit, everything clicks. Growth becomes easier. You'll know when you have it."

I get why this sounds right. We want a clear signal that says "you've made it, now scale."

**[3/8]** But here's the problem:

PMF creates a binary mindset (have it or don't) when reality is a spectrum.

I learned this the hard way. We spent 18 months "searching for PMF" instead of just improving the product iteratively.

**[4/8]** First: "PMF" is not a moment, it's a process.

Example: Slack had "PMF" in 2014 at $12M ARR. Then again at $100M ARR with a completely different buyer profile. And again at $1B when they added Enterprise.

Each stage required re-finding fit.

**[5/8]** Second: The concept paralyzes founders.

Think about it: If you don't have PMF yet, should you do marketing? Hire salespeople? Raise money?

The advice is always "no, find PMF first." But you often CAN'T find PMF without distribution.

**[6/8]** The data backs this up:

• 70% of successful startups pivot at least once (Startup Genome Report)
• Average time to "PMF" is 3+ years (not a quick unlock)
• Most unicorns had weak early metrics (Uber, Airbnb both looked terrible year 1)

The search itself is the trap.

**[7/8]** So what should you do instead?

Stop looking for PMF. Start asking:

"Can I improve retention this month?"  
"Can I make 10 customers love this?"  
"Can I charge more?"

Incremental progress > searching for a mythical threshold.

## Why This Template Works

✅ **Attention**: "Unpopular opinion" signals controversy (people slow down to read)  
✅ **Respect**: Acknowledging the conventional wisdom shows you're not just trolling  
✅ **Evidence**: Data + personal experience makes your contrarian take credible  
✅ **Engagement**: Inviting disagreement creates comments (algorithm boost)  

**Pro tip**: The best unpopular opinions are ones YOU used to believe. Your own shift from conventional → contrarian is the most compelling proof.

---

## Use Cases

**Strong fit:**
- You've seen the conventional wisdom fail repeatedly in your own experience
- You have data/examples that contradict popular belief
- The topic is widely discussed (more people care = more engagement)

**Weak fit:**
- Your opinion is actually just hot air (no evidence, no experience)
- You're contrarian for shock value (people will call you out)
- The topic is niche (unpopular opinions need an existing conversation to push against)
