# Problem-Agitate-Solution Thread Template

**When to use:** When you want to position your product/insight as the solution to a painful problem. Classic persuasion structure that works because it mirrors how people actually think when they're frustrated.

---

## The Thread Structure

**[1/8] Hook - Name the problem (be specific)**

Most [target audience] struggle with [specific problem].

I did too until I discovered [your solution/insight].

Here's the framework I use now →


**[2/8] Validate the problem (they need to nod "yes, that's me")**

You know the feeling:

• [Specific frustration 1]
• [Specific frustration 2]  
• [Specific frustration 3]

It's exhausting.


**[3/8] Agitate - make the pain real**

The worst part?

[Consequence of not solving this problem].

While your competitors [what competitors are doing], you're stuck [current bad state].


**[4/8] Introduce your solution (the "aha" moment)**

Then I realized: [your key insight].

Instead of [old way], I started [new way].

Everything changed.


**[5/8] How it works (step 1)**

Here's the system:

First: [Step 1 with specific action]

Example: [Concrete example from your experience]


**[6/8] How it works (step 2)**

Next: [Step 2 with specific action]

This is where most people mess up. [Common mistake to avoid]


**[7/8] Results (proof it works)**

Since switching to this approach:

• [Metric 1: specific number/improvement]
• [Metric 2: specific number/improvement]
• [Metric 3: specific number/improvement]

[Timeframe] of consistent results.


**[8/8] CTA - what should they do next**

Want the full framework?

I've documented:
• [Deliverable 1]
• [Deliverable 2]
• [Deliverable 3]

[Your CTA: link, DM, comment, etc.]

**[2/8]** You know the feeling:
• You send 100 emails, get 2 replies (both "not interested")
• Your "value prop" sounds like everyone else's
• You're guessing at pain points instead of knowing them

It's exhausting.

**[3/8]** The worst part? While you're burning your domain reputation on spray-and-pray, your competitors are booking 5+ demos/week from cold email. You're stuck rewriting subject lines hoping something clicks.

**[4/8]** Then I realized: nobody cares about YOUR product. They care about THEIR problem. Instead of leading with "we help you do X," I started with "I noticed you're struggling with Y."

**[5/8]** Here's the system:

First: Find 1 specific problem your ICP is publicly complaining about (Twitter, Reddit, forums).

Example: "Our sales team spends 6 hours/week updating CRM manually"

**[6/8]** Next: Send an email that ONLY talks about the problem (no pitch).

"Hey [name], saw your team is manually updating Salesforce. We had the same issue — cost us 2 lost deals last quarter because data was stale. Ever find a fix?"

**[7/8]** Since switching to this approach:

## Why This Template Works

✅ **Relatability**: Problem-first hooks get attention because they validate the reader's struggle  
✅ **Emotion**: Agitation creates urgency (pain of inaction > fear of change)  
✅ **Credibility**: Specific results > vague claims  
✅ **Clarity**: Step-by-step system is actionable, not just inspirational  

**Pro tip**: The agitate step (tweet 3) is what separates good threads from viral ones. Make them FEEL the cost of inaction.
