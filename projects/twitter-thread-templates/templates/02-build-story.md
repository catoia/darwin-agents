# Build Story Thread Template ("I Built X and Learned Y")

**When to use:** When you've just shipped something (feature, product, experiment) and want to share what you learned. Works because founders love learning from other founders' real experiences.

---

## The Thread Structure

**[1/9] Hook - The outcome (start with the punchline)**

I just spent [timeframe] building [what you built].

[Surprising result / key metric / unexpected outcome].

Here's what I learned →


**[2/9] Context - Why you built it**

Background:

We were struggling with [problem].

Tried [obvious solution], didn't work.

So I decided to [what you built instead].


**[3/9] The hypothesis - What you thought would happen**

My assumption: [what you expected]

I believed [reasoning].

Spoiler: I was [right/wrong/partially right].


**[4/9] The build - What you actually did (keep it real)**

The build took [timeframe].

Tech stack:
• [Tool/tech 1]
• [Tool/tech 2]
• [Tool/tech 3]

Hardest part: [specific challenge you hit]


**[5/9] The surprise - What you didn't expect**

Here's what shocked me:

[Unexpected finding 1]

This completely changed [what you thought you knew].


**[6/9] Key learning #1 (tactical insight)**

Lesson 1: [Specific tactical lesson]

If I did this again, I'd [specific change].

[Why this matters / how it applies more broadly]


**[7/9] Key learning #2 (strategic insight)**

Lesson 2: [Specific strategic lesson]

Most people think [common belief].

Actually: [your contrarian take based on experience]


**[8/9] The results (proof + numbers)**

After [timeframe]:

• [Metric 1]: [specific number]
• [Metric 2]: [specific number]
• [Metric 3]: [specific number]

[One-line takeaway about what this means]


**[9/9] CTA - What you're doing next / how they can learn more**

Next up: [what you're building/testing next]

Following this journey? [CTA]

Or grab the full breakdown: [link/resource]

## Example (B2B SaaS founder context)

**[1/9]** I just spent 3 weeks building a "Cancel Flow" that asks why customers churn. Reduced cancellations by 34%. Here's what I learned →

**[2/9]** Background: We were losing 8% MRR/month. Exit surveys said "too expensive" but I didn't believe it. So I built a multi-step cancel flow with real conversation, not just a survey dropdown.

**[3/9]** My assumption: Price isn't the real reason people leave. I believed if we could uncover the ACTUAL problem in the cancel moment, we could save 20%+ of churning customers. Spoiler: I was partially right.

**[4/9]** The build took 3 weeks.

Tech stack:
• Custom modal in React
• Backend logic to pause instead of cancel
• Email sequence for "paused" accounts

Hardest part: Not making it feel manipulative (dark patterns kill trust)

**[5/9]** Here's what shocked me:

47% of people who said "too expensive" actually meant "I'm not using it enough to justify the cost."

This completely changed how we think about churn (it's an engagement problem, not a pricing problem).

**[6/9]** Lesson 1: Ask "What would need to change for you to stay?" instead of "Why are you leaving?"

If I did this again, I'd add this question first (we added it week 2 and it doubled our save rate).

People will tell you how to keep them if you ask right.

**[7/9]** Lesson 2: The best churn prevention happens 30 days BEFORE they cancel.

Most people think cancel flows are last-ditch saves.

Actually: They're a diagnostic tool. We now use cancel reasons to trigger proactive outreach when we see early signals.

**[8/9]** After 6 weeks:

• 34% reduction in cancellations (8% → 5.3% monthly churn)
• 18% of cancellers accepted "pause" option
• $4.2k MRR saved (would have churned)

This was our highest-ROI project in Q1.

**[9/9]** Next up: Building the "early warning system" that flags at-risk accounts 30 days before they'd typically churn.

Following this journey? I'm sharing weekly learnings:
→ [Twitter handle]

## Why This Template Works

✅ **Curiosity**: Starting with the result (34% reduction) creates immediate interest  
✅ **Vulnerability**: Admitting your hypothesis was "partially right" builds trust  
✅ **Specificity**: Real numbers, real tools, real timeline = credible  
✅ **Actionable**: Each lesson is something readers can apply today  

**Pro tip**: The "surprise" (tweet 5) is the hook that makes people share. Find the one thing that genuinely surprised YOU — that's what will surprise them.
