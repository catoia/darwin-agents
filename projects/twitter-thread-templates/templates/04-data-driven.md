# Data-Driven Thread Template

**When to use:** When you have surprising data, research, or metrics that challenge assumptions. Works because specific numbers are inherently credible and shareable. People love data that makes them rethink what they thought they knew.

---

## Example (B2B SaaS founder context)

**[1/9]** I analyzed 347 SaaS landing pages and found something shocking:

The word "easy" DECREASES conversions by 18%.

This changes everything about SaaS copywriting. Here's what the data shows →

**[2/9]** Background:

I wanted to understand which words actually drive SaaS signups (vs what copywriters THINK works).

So I scraped 347 landing pages, tracked their conversion rates over 90 days, and analyzed word patterns.

**[3/9]** Finding 1: "Easy" appears on 63% of landing pages but correlates with LOWER conversion rates (-18% on average).

In plain English: When you say your product is "easy," fewer people sign up.

This contradicts the common belief that reducing perceived friction helps conversions.

**[4/9]** When you break it down:

• "Easy to use": -22% conversion
• "Simple and easy": -31% conversion  
• "Easy setup": -14% conversion

The pattern: "Easy" signals the product is simple/basic (not powerful). B2B buyers want capable, not simple.

**[5/9]** Finding 2: Pages with specific time-to-value statements convert 3.2x better.

Plot twist: "Connect Stripe in 4 minutes" outperforms "Easy Stripe integration" by 89%.

Example: Lemon Squeezy says "Start selling in 60 seconds" (top 5% conversion in dataset).

**[6/9]** Finding 3: Including a price on the homepage decreases trial signups by 41%.

This was the most unexpected.

Common advice is "be transparent about pricing." Data says: transparency hurts free trial conversion (though it may improve QUALITY of signups).

**[7/9]** Why this matters to you:

If you're a SaaS founder, this means:

• Replace "easy" with time-based promises ("Connect in 4 minutes")
• Use power words: "powerful," "complete," "professional"
• Hide pricing until after they try the product (for PLG motion)

**[8/9]** The data also shows what DOESN'T work:

❌ Feature lists: -12% conversion (people don't read them)
❌ "All-in-one" positioning: -8% conversion (sounds like bloatware)

Stop wasting time on these.

Following for more data-driven insights → @yourhandle

## Why This Template Works

✅ **Specificity**: Real numbers (347 landing pages, 18% decrease) = instant credibility  
✅ **Surprise**: Challenging assumptions (""easy" is bad) creates shareability  
✅ **Actionable**: Clear "stop this, start that" takeaways  
✅ **Proof**: Showing your methodology builds trust  

**Pro tip**: The best data threads have ONE stat that makes people say "wait, really?" That's tweet 1. Save your most counterintuitive finding for the hook.

## Data Sources You Can Use

**If you don't have original research:**
- Industry reports (Gartner, McKinsey, a16z State of SaaS, etc.)
- Your own product analytics (anonymized)
- Public datasets (GitHub, Kaggle, government data)
- Aggregated studies (meta-analysis of multiple papers)
- Customer surveys (your own users)

**If you DO have original research:**
- A/B test results (if sample size is meaningful)
- Customer interviews (qualitative → quantitative patterns)
- Scraped public data (always check ToS)
- Cohort analysis from your product
- Competitive analysis (pricing, features, positioning)

**Pro tip**: Original research doesn't have to be peer-reviewed academic work. "I analyzed 100 SaaS homepages" is enough if your methodology is clear.
